A vision that needed a home

In the late 1970s, leaders recognised the need for a permanent base — somewhere to run courses, host groups, and support youth development year after year. What followed was a long, determined journey: fundraising, planning, and building in stages as resources allowed.

Key milestones

From idea to decision

A formal scheme and appeal gathered momentum, moving the project from “wish” to “work”.

Site and facilities grow in stages

Facilities expanded over time — responding to need, funding, and practical opportunity.

Community effort

Volunteer time and goodwill were as important as funding — the place was built by people who cared.

Legacy

The result: a centre serving generations through courses, camps, training and adventure.

Why it still matters

The centre exists because people chose to build something that would outlast them — a practical, welcoming base for training, adventure and community. Every improvement since has continued that same purpose: making it easier for groups to grow, learn, and thrive.
